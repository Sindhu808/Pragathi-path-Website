Weaving — A Loom‑to‑Livelihood Roadmap

  1. Prepare the Loom
    Choose a frame, back‑strap, or table loom. Ensure it’s clean and solid.
  2. Warp the Loom
    Stretch vertical warp threads evenly and secure them firmly.
  3. Select Your Weft
    Pick vibrant yarns that align with local market tastes.
  4. Start Weaving
    Pass the shuttle over‑under, beating each row snug.
  5. Alternate Your Pattern
    Reverse the shed on every pass to form a strong fabric.
  6. Add Colour & Texture
    Experiment with twill, plain weave, or rya knots for flair.
  7. Finish Off
    Cut, knot and secure ends before taking the cloth off the loom.
  8. Trim & Refine
    Neatly trim stray threads; a light press makes it market‑ready.

Knitting — Your First Stitches to Sales

  1. Gather Your Materials – medium/bulky yarn, 5‑5.5 mm needles, scissors, tapestry needle.
  2. Cast On – start with a slip‑knot and the long‑tail cast‑on for strong, tidy edges.
  3. Master the Knit Stitch – in, wrap, pull through, slide off. Repeat across every row.
  4. Create Rows – switch needles at the end of each row to build garter‑stitch fabric.
  5. Bind Off Securely – knit two, pass one over; continue until a single loop remains.
  6. Weave In Tails – thread loose ends into the fabric for a professional finish.

Pottery — From Clay to Kiln

  • Materials: prepare local clay blends for strength and texture.
  • Shaping: hand‑build, throw on the wheel, or use plaster molds.
  • Firing: temperatures from 600 °C to 1 600 °C harden and waterproof the ware.
  • Types: earthenware, stoneware or porcelain – each suits different markets.
  • Decoration: glaze, paint, carve or slip‑trail unique designs.
  • Utility & Art: create cookware, storage jars or purely decorative pieces.

Basketry — Weaving Nature Into Necessity

  • Materials: willow, cane, rattan, reeds and grasses—soaked for flexibility.
  • Techniques: wicker, plaiting, twining or coiling give distinct aesthetics.
  • Uses: baskets for storage, transport, décor, mats and even garments.
  • Process: set up rigid warp spokes, weave pliable weft for the shape you need.
  • Cultural Value: each region’s style tells its own story—leverage this for niche markets.
