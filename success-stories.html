Success Stories

Saalumarada Thimmakka
Environmentalist
Saalumarada Thimmakka (born 30 June 1911) is an Indian environmentalist renowned for her extraordinary work in planting and nurturing trees, particularly along highways in Karnataka. Despite having no formal education and working as a casual laborer, Thimmakka’s dedication to the environment has made her a global inspiration
Bina Devi
Mushroom Cultivator
Bina Devi, from Munger, Bihar, is known as the “Mushroom Lady” or ‘Mushroom Mahila’. Despite limited resources, she learned mushroom cultivation and grew her first batch under her bed. Once she mastered the technique, she trained and inspired over 1,500 women in 105 villages to take up mushroom farming, providing them with a sustainable source of income and self-reliance. Her efforts have popularized mushroom production in her region and empowered many rural women to become entrepreneurs
Kanika Talukdar
Vermicompost Entrepreneur
Widowed at 27 with a four-month-old daughter, Kanika Talukdar from Nalbari, Assam, started her own vermicompost business, Jay Vermi Compost, with just Rs 500 and 1 kg of earthworms. Using locally available materials, she built her business from scratch. Today, she earns up to Rs 3.5 lakh per month, sells on Amazon and Flipkart, and supplies to farmers across eastern India. Kanika’s journey from adversity to prosperity is a beacon of hope for rural women.
Karishma Choyal
Flower Entrepreneur
Karishma Choyal and her husband started a flower cultivation business in Bhagsur village, Madhya Pradesh, after returning home during the COVID-19 pandemic. With no prior experience, they learned farming, built a brand, and expanded their business with the help of local entrepreneurship programs. Today, “Powan Flowers and Decoration Enterprise” is well-known in the region, and Karishma is a role model for other rural women, using social media to market her products.
